Brief

CW1 – Practical Exercise Vector Illustration

Project Brief

The task was to design a vector illustration for a series of A4 posters for MaltaDoors, a visual blog showcasing the colorful and unique facades and doorways of the Maltese Islands. As a graphic designer, I was required to conduct visual research by photographing at least 25 images of doors, street-scapes, shops, garages, and other interesting street furniture to inspire the artwork. One door was then selected for conversion into a vector illustration using Adobe Illustrator, where the design elements were analyzed and simplified to focus on the essential visual elements. The final deliverables included a presentation of the visual research and sketch in PDF format, as well as the A4 artwork in PDF format.

Software

Adobe Photoshop

Adobe Photoshop is an essential part of my workflow when it comes to editing and enhancing my photos.
Used for:

  • Adjusting exposure, contrast, and color balance to correct issues with the original photo
  • Cropping and resizing images to create a more focused composition
  • Removing unwanted objects using the clone stamp or healing brush tools
  • Adding or removing people or objects from an image using layer masks
  • Using advanced features such as the content-aware fill or perspective warp to manipulate images in creative ways

Procreate

Procreate is a digital painting app that allows for flexibility, creativity, and efficiency in the sketching process.
Used for:

  • Procreate was used for initial sketching of door design
  • Allowed for flexibility and easy editing in digital format
  • Helped to create a clean and focused illustration
  • Allowed for quick and easy experimentation with colors
  • High-resolution export allowed for easy integration into final design
  • Saved time and resources compared to traditional sketching methods

Adobe Illustrator

Adobe Illustrator is an essential tool for creating precise, scalable vector graphics and designs.
Used for:

  • Illustrator tools used for intricate details like wrought ironwork and floral motifs, such as shape builder tool and stroke options.
  • Consistent colors with palette feature, easy access to frequently used colors, and consistency with the original door design.
  • Added gradients and shadows for realism and better design.
  • Scalable vector illustration allowed for the poster design to be printed in various sizes.

Process

Photographing the Doors

For the commissioned project for MaltaDoors, I photographed various doors found in Gudja, Malta, which displayed unique and distinct designs with bright colors, intricate wrought ironwork, and decorative patterns such as floral motifs and geometric shapes. During the photography session, I took great care to capture every detail of the doors, aiming for symmetry and balance in my shots. I was struck by the passion and pride the residents of Gudja had for their traditional architecture and their appreciation for the doors’ artistic value. The doors were not simply functional elements of the buildings, but also reflected the rich cultural heritage and history of the island.

Sketching the Chosen Door

After photographing the doors, I selected one with a remarkable design that caught my eye and proceeded to sketch it in Procreate. During the sketching process, I carefully deliberated on which elements to retain and which to eliminate to create a clean design that highlights the key features of the door.

Creating the Vector Illustration

Next, I created a vector illustration of the chosen door. This was a challenging task as I needed to ensure that the intricate details of the door translated well into a poster format. To achieve the wrought ironwork for the window above the door, I used Adobe Illustrator’s tools, such as strokes with decorative floral borders, rotated and reflected from a central point. I also utilized the shape builder tool to add or cut out shapes to achieve the door panels and window arch. To ensure consistency and efficiency in the color process, I saved colors as a palette in Illustrator. In addition, I added gradients to enhance realism and shadows to improve the overall design.

One advantage of creating the illustration in a scalable vector format is that it can be resized without losing its quality or clarity. Even though the final version was in A4 format, the flexibility of a scalable vector allows the poster to be printed in various sizes, from A4 to larger formats, as needed.
